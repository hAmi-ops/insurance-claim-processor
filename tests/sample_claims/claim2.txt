Description of Incident:
During the severe winter storm that affected central Texas on January 25, 2024, a water pipe in the attic of my home burst due to freezing temperatures that dropped to 8°F overnight. The pipe, which is part of the home's main water supply line running through the attic space, cracked at a joint fitting when ice expanded within the pipe.

I discovered the damage on the morning of January 26 when I noticed water stains on the ceiling of the primary bedroom and heard dripping sounds from above. Upon inspection, I found that water had been flowing from the burst pipe for an estimated 8-10 hours overnight. The water had saturated the attic insulation, soaked through the ceiling drywall in three rooms, and pooled on the hardwood flooring in the primary bedroom and adjacent hallway.

I immediately shut off the main water supply and contacted a licensed plumber (Lone Star Plumbing, License #M-41235) who performed emergency repairs to stop the leak. I also contacted ServiceMaster for emergency water extraction and drying services, which began the same day.

Damage Assessment:
- Attic insulation (approximately 400 sq ft): saturated, requires full replacement

- Hardwood flooring: primary bedroom and hallway (approximately 350 sq ft) - warped and buckling, requires replacement
- Primary bedroom carpet padding: saturated, requires replacement
- Drywall mud and paint: all affected ceilings and upper walls
